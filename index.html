Mona Zhu


Cognitive scientist by day, data wrangler by trade. I recently obtained my PhD from the University of Waterloo in September, 2020. Currently, I work as a Research Scientist at the Brain, Attention & Reality Lab looking at ways to examine individual differences in people's eye- and head-movements via VR study data. Prior to this, I was a Research Intern at BEworks where I developed and validated various behavioural tasks and metrics that could help to inform hiring decisions.

In my PhD work, I studied human spatial organization, such as how and when we decide to rearrange items (or ourselves) in space, what drives these decisions, individual differences in how we may choose to engage in spatial organization in everyday search tasks.
