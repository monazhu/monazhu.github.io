Mona Zhu


Cognitive scientist by day, data wrangler by trade. I recently obtained my PhD from the University of Waterloo in September, 2020. In my PhD work, I studied human spatial organization, such as how and when we decide to rearrange items (or ourselves) in space, what drives these decisions, and individual differences in how we may choose to engage in spatial organization in everyday search tasks.

Currently, I work as an Analyst at BEworks, where I was previously an intern. I divide my time evenly between client-facing work where help clients identify psychological barriers in the behavioural problems they present to us; the other half of the time, I am heavily involve in data analytics, primarily using various clustering algorithms to identify key population segments and building a pipeline to streamline the analysis for other associates on the team. Prior to this, I worked briefly as a Research Scientist at the Brain, Attention & Reality Lab looking at ways to examine individual differences in people's eye- and head-movements via VR study data.
